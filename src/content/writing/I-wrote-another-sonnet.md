<h2>I wrote another sonnet does it make you want to love me?</h2>

*an ekphrastic dialogue*

*after “The Large Glass” by Marcel Duchamp*

<div class="stanza">
even a bride stripped bare by her bachelors can’t avoid being <br />
made into a machine herself. so opposite motion awaits <br />
perpetual. how else to maintain an arrangement unpredictable <br />
against fate except to grin and bear it as light through glass. <br />
fractures wouldn’t matter except routine expectation shapes <br />
desire. proffering noble declarations in traditional styles <br />
they follow beaten paths to drum what little life remains <br />
into dust. burrowing in popular crypts to ransack corpses <br />
with industry I lay among their dead and shout decay. and still <br />
what tattered pieces might you discover if forced to descend <br />
to where I dwell. inscribed there solitary darkness suggests <br />
your presence as a cast shadow holds up a figure to the sun.<br />
although such a staged dance of expressions can’t convince <br />
you know my useless words would give way to so much more.
</div>
<br /><br />
<div class="stanza">
sigh. no matter how these fissures produce such playful sleights<br />
your turns of phrase still isolate. unable to bask in my light when <br />
at this height even waterfalls drain through sieves. you become<br />
heavy with holes. a din in dark spaces at the mouth of a bottle.<br />
in continuous suspension your ideas scatter like rain tears through <br />
an overturned parasol. mere stones in cemeteries uniforms show<br />
devotion akin to gears grinding down teeth in machines. runners<br />
chasing their finish line never tire although open scissors suggest <br />
shortcuts to abrupt endings might yet be possible. and I blossom<br />
nonetheless without your erotic litanies fit for priests blowing gas<br />
in the dark. in mirrors I witness so much more than you and your <br />
architectonic delusions could ever command. anyways electricity <br />
transmits by current not osmosis. and though it might ignite sparks <br />
burning buildings to the ground your love to me tastes of gasoline. 
</div>
